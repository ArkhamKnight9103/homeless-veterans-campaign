Our Purpose

 Our organization is here to promote awareness for homeless veterans and their families that struggle with everyday life when they come home. Many veterans come back with missing limbs, PTSD, and other disabilities that makes finding jobs and being apart of a community difficult. We hope to bring awareness and get more people involved in helping those who fought for our freedom. Donating to various charities that focus on rehabilitating veterans to become productive members of society is a great way that you can help, but money is not the only way to help those in need. You can donate old clothes, canned foods, and other necessities that you may have no use for anymore. Simply spreading awareness by participating in events that are held by these charities is a great way to get involved in the movement by donating your time. These great men and women fought for our country to protect our rights and privileges, but are rewarded by coming home to a family in debt and struggling to put food on the table. When we need them the most they were there for us, so it's only right that we stand by them in their time of need.

Events

Stand Downs are events usually held for one to three days, that provide veterans with services, food, and shelter. Veterans are also given referals to other services and helped through the process of getting back on their feet financially. For more information or a list of Stand Downs near you, read the Offical VA Page on Stand Downs.

List of Charities that Help Veterans

  1. SHV - "Support Homeless Veterans"

     SHV helps bring back quality of life to veterans by offering financial assistence to veterans.

  2. AMVETS - "Assistance with getting back to a normal life"

     AMVETS helps veterans get back into a normal life.

  3. Hire Heroes USA - "Transforming Military Service into Civilian Success"

     Hire Heros USA assists veterans get hired

  4. Fisher House Foundation - "A Network of Comfort Homes"

     Fisher House Foundation is where veteran families can stay while a loved one is receiving treatment.

  5. Operation Homefront - "Serving American Military Families"

     Operation Homefront helps veteran families by providing food, home and auto assistance.
